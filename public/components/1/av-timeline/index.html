1. 2009
    Google (Waymo Precursor)
    Project Chauffeur Genesis
    Secret autonomous vehicle project begins within Google X, laying foundational work for what would become Waymo.
    Marks the early, strategic investment by a tech giant into self-driving technology, shaping future R&D.
  2. 2015
    Tesla
    Autopilot (Level 2) Launched
    Tesla releases its initial "Autopilot" system, a Level 2 Advanced Driver Assistance System (ADAS), to consumer vehicles.
    Popularizes ADAS features, initiates large-scale data collection, and sparks public discourse on vehicle automation.
  3. 2016
    Waymo
    Waymo Becomes Independent
    Google's self-driving car project is spun out as Waymo, an independent subsidiary under Alphabet Inc.
    Signals a shift towards commercialization and product development beyond pure research, intensifying industry focus.
  4. 2018
    Waymo
    "Waymo One" Early Rider Program
    Launches its first commercial robotaxi trial service in Phoenix, Arizona, initially with human safety drivers.
    Represents a crucial step in testing real-world robotaxi operations and gathering public feedback.
  5. 2020
    Waymo
    Fully Driverless Service (Phoenix)
    Waymo opens its fully driverless (Level 4) robotaxi service to the general public in the suburbs of Phoenix.
    A landmark achievement: one of the first truly driverless commercial services available to the public.
  6. 2023
    Cruise
    Major Safety Incident & Pause
    A significant safety incident involving a pedestrian in San Francisco leads to a nationwide operational pause and recall for Cruise.
    Highlights persistent safety challenges, increases regulatory scrutiny, and impacts public trust in AV technology.
  7. 2025
    Industry Wide
    Increased Deployments & Regulatory Focus
    Waymo announces significant fleet expansion. Aurora launches commercial driverless trucking. NHTSA issues new federal guidelines.
    Marks a period of accelerating commercialization attempts alongside evolving, more specific regulatory frameworks.
