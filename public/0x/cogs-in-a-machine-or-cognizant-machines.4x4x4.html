Scene One: Familiarity's Phantom

When a new moment feels like an old script's rerun.

This conceptual bar chart illustrates the cognitive conflict inherent in the common déjà vu experience clearly. A high bar represents subjective "Familiarity" alongside objective novelty, with a "Conflict Line" between them.

The term déjà vu describes that unique, fleeting feeling of experiencing a novel situation again. This moment seems to inexplicably mirror a phantom past, which creates an uncanny sensation for any individual, an error in the mind's ongoing internal production. This is an unreliable narration.[1][2]

This cognitive dissonance, this "glitch" in our perceived reality, has long captivated many philosophers. Its mechanisms remain unsolved.[5][6]

This internal conflict between knowing a scene is new and feeling it is old is very central. These are the main neural actors.[17][18]
